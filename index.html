Spicy Pork Spareribs

Sweet and Sticky, Chinese spareribs are a favorite finger food. These are steeped in a flavorful marinade before roasting, which makes them among the tastiest ribs ever.

Serves 4

  • 16 baby back ribs, total weight about 1 ½ lbs, trimmed of excess fat
  • 1 tsp finely chopped garlic
  • 1 tsp five-spice powder (page 7)
  • 1 tb soft brown sugar
  • 1 tsp chili sauce
  • 2 tbs dark soy sauce
  • About ⅔ cup Chinese Stock (page 12) or water
  • Lettuce leaves for serving
  • Shredded red bell pepper and scallion strips for garnish
  1. Place the ribs in a baking dish. Mix together the garlic, ginger, five-spice powder, sugar, chili, soy, and hoisin saices, and pour over the ribs. Cover and marinate at room temperature for 2-3 hours, or in the refrigerator for 5-6 hours.
  2. Sprinkle 6 tbs stock or water over the ribs and stir to mix well. Roast the ribs and stir to mix well.
  3. Arrange a bed of lettuce leaves on a serving platter.
